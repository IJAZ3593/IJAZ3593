## 🚀 About Me

I'm **Malik Ijaz**, a backend developer passionate about building systems that solve real-world problems.

My journey into software engineering didn't begin with perfect grades or elite resources. It began with long commutes, financial pressure, failed exams, and countless nights teaching myself how computers actually work.

I spent years optimizing crypto mining rigs, writing Python automation scripts, and experimenting with backend systems. What once looked like a distraction eventually became the foundation of my engineering mindset.

Today, I focus on:

* 🔧 Backend Architecture
* ⚡ Automation Systems
* 🐧 Linux & Open Source
* 🌐 Web Technologies
* 📊 Data Pipelines
* 🛡 Cybersecurity-Oriented Tools

My goal is simple:

> Build systems that remove friction from people's lives and create opportunities for those who are often overlooked.

---

# 📖 The Distance Between Two Points

At 2:47 AM, I deleted an opportunity that many people dream of.

A fully funded pathway to Dubai, backed by a scholarship and a job opportunity.

I wasn't rejected.

I rejected myself.

My grades no longer reflected my capabilities. I had spent too much time building systems and too little time satisfying academic expectations.

That decision became the first major regret of my life.

Years later, after failing subjects, commuting 25 kilometers daily, and struggling to balance family responsibilities with education, I discovered something important:

**Failure is data.**

Every mistake contained information.

Every setback revealed a system that could be improved.

That mindset eventually led me into backend engineering, automation, hackathons, and leadership roles where I now help other students avoid the same obstacles I faced.

Today, I don't build systems to escape reality.

I build systems to improve it.
